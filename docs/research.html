თეორიული საფუძველი / Theoretical anchor

The Compensatory Input Hypothesis

Georgian's verb system is templatic and obligatorily polypersonal, with up to nine ordered slots encoding preverb, subject and object agreement, version vowel, root, thematic suffix, and screeve-based agreement. Unlike fusional or isolating systems, Georgian's morphology offers caregivers no simplification path: minimal verb forms still carry obligatory inflection. We hypothesize that caregivers compensate not by reducing morphological complexity but by elevating paradigmatic density — clustering forms of the same lemma in proximal discourse positions so that morphological contrasts are made visible through variation sets.

Polypersonalism in action

Person markers appear as prefixes or suffixes depending on the screeve series and transitivity. The following examples (drawn from Tsintsadze et al., 2026) illustrate the simultaneous encoding of subject and object person within a single verb form:

(1) ts'er-s write-3SG.SBJ (S)he writes
(2) m-ts'er-s 1SG.OBJ-write-3SG.SBJ (S)he writes to me
(3) m-i-ts'er-s 1SG.OBJ-VER-write-3SG.SBJ (S)he writes for me

In m-ts'er-s, the prefix m- marks first-person singular object while the suffix -s marks third-person singular subject. The version vowel -i- in (3) introduces a benefactive reading without altering the agreement pattern.
